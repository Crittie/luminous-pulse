## Post 21 — Grounding Practice: "The Name-It Practice"

Try this tonight before bed.

Grab a piece of paper and a pen. Not your phone. Paper.

Write this at the top: "Right now, I am carrying..."

Then finish the sentence. Don't edit. Don't perform. Don't make it sound good.

Just write what's true.

"Right now, I am carrying the fear that I peaked at my last job."
"Right now, I am carrying the shame of needing help."
"Right now, I am carrying anger at a company I gave everything to."

Neuroscience says: the act of naming an emotion in writing reduces its intensity by up to 50%. It's called "affect labeling" — and it works because your prefrontal cortex takes over from your amygdala when you put feelings into words.

You don't have to solve what you write. You just have to name it. That's the first exhale.
