### Competitive Positioning Assessment

**vs. Calm/Headspace:** Luminous Pulse's copy is warmer and more personal. Where Calm says "breathe" over a lake, Luminous Pulse says "breathe — because your chest has been tight since that meeting and you haven't told anyone." Specificity is the competitive advantage.

**vs. Mark Manson/Brene Brown:** Closest to Brown in willingness to name vulnerability, but more specific — Brown speaks to universal vulnerability; Luminous Pulse speaks to career displacement specifically. Writing quality is at a professional published level. The content quality is there; distribution needs to catch up.

**vs. Generic affirmation accounts:** Different league entirely. Those accounts post generic typography over sunsets. Luminous Pulse posts "My layoff is not a performance review. It is a business decision that happened to me." The specificity and lowercase sanctuary voice make this feel like an entirely different genre.

### Missing Content (Post-Launch Priorities)

1. **Post-purchase email sequence for $37 product** — What happens after someone buys? Buyer's remorse risk without onboarding.
2. **Weekly newsletter strategy** — After Day 21, subscribers enter "weekly newsletter" but no content strategy is defined.
3. **FAQ/About page** — What is Luminous Pulse? Is it religious? Is it therapy? Reduce friction.
4. **Supporter audience content** — "How to support someone going through displacement." High-search, low-competition topic.
5. **Referral/sharing mechanism** — No formal "send this to someone who needs it" flow beyond a P.S. mention.
6. **TikTok-specific content adaptation** — All video scripts target IG Reels. TikTok has different norms.
